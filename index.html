Test I Ever Had

It's clear that sex education across the U.S. is not adequate. According to Planned Parenthood, sex education is shaped at the state level, which leads to teaching based on the beliefs of the current state legislators in power. Lawmakers can enforce lesson plans that omit information, are not inclusive of a diverse spectrum of sexualities and genders, and are biased towards more conservative beliefs.

In some states, it's difficult enough to obtain a cohesive knowledge on safe sex and options for contraception. Forget learning about sexually transmitted infections, when to get tested for them, and how to get tested for them.
